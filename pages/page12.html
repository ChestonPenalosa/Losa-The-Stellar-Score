Straight to Hangar

There should be just enough time for you to get back to your ship and leave before anyone even realizes what just happened. Sachi doesn't like this somewhat risky route as it feels like a one-way ticket to prison but appreciates the possible lack of attention. Dom yawns in boredom and simply just starts thinking about what to do with the device once you've escaped.

You all sneak out of the vault area and quickly head into the nearby crowd, remaining light on your feet, making your way through the crowds and towards the hangar. You steer clear of any guards and hide your faces from any surveillance. However, you slow down, realizing that you are somewhat lost in the sea of people. Fortunately, Sachi moves forward, taking the lead by pulling out her map of the area. Before you know it, you all arrive at the hangar and find your ship. You all hop in, ignite the engine, and swiftly fly out of the premises. Once out, you hear a subtle alarm going off. Surely enough, you check the ship's rear-view cameras, and the security alarms are blaring off right after you left. You, Sachi, and Dom all celebrate knowing that you've possibly pulled off one of the greatest heists in the galaxy, with a powerful prototype in your possession.

Now comes the question to finally cap off this heist: what do you do with the prototype? Sachi thinks that you should keep the device. None of you really know what the device is, does, or how it works, but with some time and testing you should be able to utilize the possible powers it grants. Or it could be a dud that simply isn't ready to be used. After some thinking, but not much, Dom suggests selling the device. He doesn't have the patience to understand the device, but he knows the reputation it has across the galaxy. He starts babbling on about all the riches and gold you all could be bathed in once sold, all the expensive ships and explosives he could buy. But a small thought crosses your mind: what if you just destroy it? Whether the rumors of the sheer power it holds are true or not, should a device like this be trusted in your hands, or even anyone's hands?
